There are persistent myths about data centre heat reuse that need addressing. The most common: that it's too expensive, too complex, and too unreliable to work at scale.

None of these are true.

The economics of heat reuse have shifted dramatically in the last five years. Energy costs have risen, regulation has tightened, and the technology has matured. What was once a nice-to-have is becoming a competitive necessity.

The real barrier isn't technical — it's cultural. Data centre operators think of themselves as technology companies, not energy companies. Breaking that mental model is the first step to unlocking billions in wasted value.
